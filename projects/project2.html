Sacred Ground
2021

Sacred Ground is an original branding and packaging design concept for a line of fertilizers specifically marketed for indoor plants. Their eco-conscious products allude to the spiritual connection between humans and the natural world and are aimed at Millenial and Gen-Z consumers whose lifestyles are more suited to reverently raising a curated collection of houseplants than caring for large flowerbeds.

Typography provided by Ohno Type Co.


SERVICES

Branding Design, Illustration

TYPOGRAPHY

The brand's wordmark utilizes Eckmannpsych, capitalizing on the recent trend towards Jugend-ish fonts that hasn't yet hit the commercial mainstream. The psychedelic futurist font and bold color palette inject life, authenticity, and personality into the brand's visual design, but remain balanced by the use of a clean modern sans-serif throughout.


ILLUSTRATIONS

The detailed hand-drawn illustrations on Sacred Ground's packaging and in their marketing materials allude to traditional Scandanavian folk art, Mexican Otomi textiles, and religious and mystical elements—including the Eye of Ra, open hands, and arched frames reminiscent of Byzantine cathedrals—which serve to reinforce the brand's philosophy regarding the spirtual impacts of plants.

INSPIRATION

I conceptualized this project as a way to challenge myself to reach beyond the typical portfolio project and create a sincere and deeply-rooted identity for a less-than-palatable product. After reading about Craig Dubitsky's success at taking toothpaste from a commodity to a beloved brand with Hello Products, I was inspired to adapt fertilizer—an unpleasant, environmentally damaging, bulky product that exists in a space with nearly zero product differentiation—to serve the constantly increasing demand for indoor plants.

While creating the visual identity for Sacred Ground, I pushed myself to break from conventional design wisdom and experiment. The UX side of me does flinch a little bit at the readability of the wordmark, and the palette of soft lavender, pumpkin orange, and the acidic neon yellow still makes me shiver. Yet, the final result, a brand I'm confident would stand out in the gardening aisle or draw you in at a boutique florist, is just the right mix of rebellious and traditional for a youthful and purpose-driven fertilizer brand.
